Should the brain autonomously research my clippings?

The desire: "Investigate/research the topics in my clippings over time, in off-hours — do something with the articles & resources that just sit there; sometimes they might connect to something."

The stakes: ~89 saved notes that go dormant after filing; a free local model + idle off-hours compute; an existing forward-only connections engine — versus a 48GB bloat death (external fetch is the bloat vector) and a habit of abandoning ambitious tools.

Council Verdict

Where the council agrees

No external web fetch (4/5 advisors + every reviewer) — it's the literal bloat vector. Local model only (free; no unattended-cron cost runaway). The problem is processing debt / write-only accumulation, not a lack of research. Don't generate a new pile of reports nobody reads.

Where it clashes

Expansionist's "autonomous synthesis / latent book" vs everyone — resolved against (unanimous: the rationalization that rebuilds the tar pit). Then Executor's new Resurface-DATE.md vs "no new output" — resolved by a reviewer's insight: keep the mechanism, change the output → write the connection in-place on the note + ride the one best hit on a channel that already reaches you.

Blind spots the peer review caught

(1) Verify the existing connections/nightly output is actually read before adding a loop, or it's another corpse. (2) Write enrichment in-place, not as new files — avoid the "write-only brain." (3) Capture-side friction may be the higher-leverage lever.

The recommendation NOT a research agent a bounded Librarian

Build the Off-Hours Librarian: a nightly, local-model-only pass that reverses the connections mtime filter to find the single strongest link between a dormant clipping and your active work, writes a one-line verdict in-place on the dormant note (🔗 resurfaced — connects to [[active]] · relevant? / resolved / orphaned), and feeds the ONE best hit into the Morning Brief that already pushes to your phone. No external fetch, no new report files, no cloud, guard-gated, corpus size stays flat. Reuses run_connections + the delivery you already have — not a new subsystem.

The one thing to do first

Prove the channel before building the engine. Add one "dormant clipping that connects to today's work" line to the existing brief, generated nightly by the local model. If within 2–3 weeks you act on even one resurfaced clipping, expand. If not, the desire was about guilt — and the answer is friction at capture, not a research engine.

Full advisor responses

The Contrarian processing debt
The desire is a displacement behavior — fetching new info instead of processing what you have. The 48GB death was a processing-debt problem, not storage; autonomous research adds debt faster than you clear it and the output goes unread → corpse. Local models on research tasks produce confident nonsense → ghost citations corrupt your own memory. "Serendipitous resurfacing" already half-exists in the connections engine and isn't maxed. The version that earns its right is NOT research: a weekly digest of which Literature notes have ZERO connections after 30 days. Force the bottleneck into view; make the human process before the machine generates.
The First Principles Thinker it's guilt, not research
The user doesn't want research — they want to stop feeling guilty about saved things. Every clipping is a broken promise; research generates MORE content around it. Reframe: not "research this topic?" but "is this clipping still load-bearing?" First version: zero fetch, zero new content; one weekly local-model call per clipping comparing its core claim against Open-Loops + recent notes → a verdict (still relevant / resolved / orphaned) as a tag + one sentence. No new notes. Close the loop on why it was saved.
The Expansionist bold dissent · unanimous blind spot
The undervalued upside: this quietly assembles your intellectual autobiography — a latent book. Every clipping was a vote; cross-pollinated nightly by the free local model, they reveal chapter structures you never consciously assembled. "Synthesis-as-first-draft": wake to a 400-word synthesis of three notes that cohere. Compounding personal insight; the corpus is irreproducible = the moat. (Reviewers, 5/5: a hope, not a design — no stopping condition, no success metric, no mechanism to prevent the 48GB death; the exact rationalization that produced the prior bloat.)
The Outsider fix the input
89 unread articles = a filing habit, not a learning habit. An AI that reads them and sends nightly reports is just a 90th thing you won't read. When two articles "connect," you feel briefly interested, file it, nothing changes. The night-research idea solves for the feeling of being on top of material, not using it. Real fix: friction on INPUT — delete one to save one; or force one article/week to the top of the screen, write one sentence before you can dismiss it. Ask why you save things you don't read.
The Executor strongest · 4/5
Add run_resurface() to vault_ai.py modeled on the existing run_connections(), but sort by oldest mtime (most dormant), take the bottom ~20, ask the local model which connect to recently-touched notes. Use ollama gemma3:12b only (free, 128k ctx; a cloud model in an unattended cron at $0.05/run is dangerous). Skip external fetch — you have 89 notes you've never connected; that IS the research. Wire into the existing nightly operator (no new cron). ~40-line diff. Resurface first; internet later.
